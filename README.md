# US-Coffee-Shop-Sales-Analysis
### This Repository has US Coffee Shop Sales Analysis projects.

_"From the First Sip to the Last Sale — Every Cup Has a Story Worth Analyzing"_

## Project Overview
US Coffee Shop Sales Analysis Dashboard is a comprehensive retail analytics project built entirely in Microsoft Excel and Power Bi, analyzing transactional sales data from a multi-location coffee shop chain operating across three New York City neighborhoods — Astoria, Hell's Kitchen, and Lower Manhattan. The dataset spans 6 months (January to June) with 149,116 total footfall transactions generating $698,812.33 in total revenue.
The project features two fully interactive dashboard views — a Light Theme Dashboard for clean, professional presentation and a Dark Theme Dashboard for modern, visually striking display — both powered by Month and Weekday slicers for dynamic time-based filtering.
The analysis covers hourly order patterns, product category performance, order size distribution, store-level footfall and sales comparison, top 5 revenue-generating products, weekday order trends, and per-customer billing metrics. This type of analysis is directly relevant to coffee shop operations management, menu pricing strategy, staff scheduling, inventory planning, and marketing campaign timing.


## Business Objectives
  - Analyze overall coffee shop sales performance
  - Understand customer footfall patterns
  - Identify peak order hours and busy weekdays
  - Track store-wise revenue and order distribution
  - Evaluate product category contribution
  - Identify top-performing products
  - Improve operational and marketing decisions

# Key Insights
1) Peak Hours Are 9–10 AM — The Morning Rush Window Is Critical
  - Order volume peaks sharply between 9 AM (25,370 orders) and 10 AM (26,713 orders), with the absolute peak at 10 AM. Volume begins ramping from 7 AM (19,449) and sustains relatively well through 8 AM (25,197) and 9 AM. After 11 AM, there is a steep and sustained drop, with orders falling to ~12,000–13,000 through the afternoon. This 2-hour morning window (9–10 AM) is the most commercially critical period of the entire operating day.


2) Coffee Is the #1 Revenue Category at 39%
  - Coffee generates $269,952 (39% of total revenue) — nearly double the next highest category. Tea follows at 28% ($196,406), and Bakery contributes 12% ($82,316). Drinking Chocolate accounts for 10% ($72,416). At the bottom, Packaged Chocolate ($4,408), Flavours ($8,409), and Branded ($13,607) are minimal contributors. The portfolio is clearly coffee and tea dominated, with significant upsell opportunity in lower-performing categories.


3) Barista Espresso Is the Single Best-Selling Product at $91,406
  - Among all product types, Barista Espresso leads with $91,406.20 in revenue — more than double the second-ranked Brewed Chai Tea ($77,081.95). This is followed by Gourmet Brewed Coffee ($70,034.60), Hot Chocolate ($72,416), and Brewed Black Tea ($47,932). Barista Espresso's dominance suggests a strong customer preference for premium espresso-based drinks.


4) Hell's Kitchen Is the Top Revenue Store at $236,511
  - Among the three locations, Hell's Kitchen leads with $236,511.17 in sales from 50,735 orders, followed by Astoria ($232,243.91 / 50,599 orders) and Lower Manhattan ($230,057.25 / 47,782 orders). The stores are remarkably balanced in both footfall and revenue — with less than 3% variance between the highest and lowest revenue store — indicating consistent operational performance across all three locations.


5) Order Size Split Is Evenly Three-Way — Large, Regular, and "Not Defined"
  - The size distribution shows an almost even three-way split: Large (30%), Regular (31%), Not Defined (30%), and Small (9%). The high "Not Defined" category at 30% is a significant data quality issue — this likely represents products without a standard size classification (such as bakery items, coffee beans, or branded merchandise) that need better categorization for accurate size-based analysis.


6) Weekday Sales Are Remarkably Consistent — No Single Day Dominates
  - Order volume across weekdays is strikingly consistent: Wednesday leads at 21,421 orders, followed by Saturday (21,695) and Sunday (21,579). Monday (21,141) and Thursday (20,816) are at the lower end but within a narrow range. The total weekly bill follows a similar pattern with Wednesday ($101,288) and Sunday ($101,174) at the top. This near-uniform weekday distribution means the business cannot rely on a single "big day" — every day matters equally.


7) Evening Sales Drop Sharply After 7 PM
  - Order volume falls from 12,700 at 5 PM to 10,826 at 6 PM, 8,595 at 7 PM, and crashing to just 880 orders at 8 PM — an 89% drop from peak hours. This dramatic evening dropoff suggests either an early closing time, a residential area mismatch with customer habits, or insufficient evening promotions. The post-3 PM period is a consistent weak zone throughout the day.


8) Lower Manhattan Has the Lowest Footfall but Competitive Revenue
  - Lower Manhattan has the lowest footfall at 47,782 orders — nearly 3,000 fewer than the other two stores — yet its revenue of $230,057 is only 2.7% below Hell's Kitchen's top position. This suggests Lower Manhattan customers have a higher average spend per transaction, possibly due to a more premium product mix or higher-priced orders — making it the most revenue-efficient store on a per-customer basis.



# Business Recommendations
1) Optimize Morning Operations - (Morning hours experience the highest traffic.)
  - Increase staffing during 7–10 AM
  - Prepare high-demand products in advance
  - Introduce express pickup options


2) Focus on High-Revenue Products - (Products like Espresso and Chai Tea drive significant revenue.)
  - Create combo offers
  - Improve in-store promotion
  - Launch premium variants


3) Increase Average Order Value - (Average orders per person are relatively low (1.44).)
  - Cross-sell bakery items
  - Bundle beverages with snacks
  - Introduce loyalty rewards


4) Location-Based Strategy - (Different stores show different customer spending patterns.)
  - Customize promotions by location
  - Analyze customer demographics
  - Optimize pricing regionally


5) Enhance Customer Retention - (Footfall is strong, but repeat purchase optimization can improve revenue further.)
  - Introduce loyalty programs
  - Offer subscription coffee plans
  - Launch personalized offers

## Conclusion
The Coffee Shop Sales Dashboard provides a comprehensive view of sales performance, customer behavior, and operational trends across multiple store locations. By analyzing key metrics such as total sales, customer footfall, product demand, peak ordering hours, and store-wise performance, the dashboard helps management make data-driven decisions to improve business efficiency and profitability. The analysis reveals that coffee products contribute the highest share of revenue, while morning hours generate the maximum customer traffic, highlighting opportunities for targeted staffing and promotional strategies. Additionally, insights into customer preferences, order sizes, and top-selling products support better inventory planning and menu optimization. Store-level comparisons further help identify high-performing locations and areas requiring improvement. Overall, this project demonstrates how business intelligence and data visualization can transform raw sales data into actionable insights, enabling coffee shop management to enhance customer experience, optimize operations, increase revenue, and support long-term business growth.
